Behind the Watt · brand & design system · v1.1

behindthe watt

One idea holds everything together: the meter line. A thin vertical rule that separates announcements from verified fact. Left of the line — what they said. Right of the line — what the permits show. Editorial journal for trust, public-record details for character.

01 · The meter line

The core brand device. A 2px ink vertical rule. It appears in exactly four places — never as decoration.

Where it lives

1. The wordmark separator. 2. The hero divider (claims left, facts right). 3. The zero axis of every chart — especially Time-to-Power bars. 4. Blockquote rule on "as filed" excerpts. Anywhere else: don't.

DO

Grayed, right-aligned content left of the line. Ink or green, left-aligned right of the line. The line is always full-height of its block, always 2px, always ink.

DON'T

Don't use it as a generic divider between random sections. Don't color it. Don't make it dashed. If both sides are the same kind of content, use a hairline (1px, --line) instead.

02 · Logo

Wordmark: IBM Plex Mono medium, lowercase. "behind" in ink-3, the meter line, "the watt" in ink. The brand speaks the voice of the record — the wordmark is set in mono because the project itself is a quote from public filings. Short form: btw., lowercase with a period. Mark: the meter line beside a W.

Rules

Always lowercase. Never letterspace the wordmark. Clear space = height of the W on all sides. The mark never appears without the line. btw. keeps its period — it's a sentence, not an acronym.

The one rule

Green means "we verified it." Red means "the record shows a violation." Never use them for anything else — no green buttons, no red sale banners. The palette is the credibility.

04 · Typography

Three voices. The journal speaks serif, the interface speaks sans, the record speaks mono.

IBM Plex Mono · 400/500 · 13–11pxDOCKET 26-0169-EL-BLN · FILED 2026-03-27 · 296 MW — anything that quotes the record

Rules

Serif is reserved for numbers that matter and headlines that get quoted. Mono is reserved for facts traceable to a document: IDs, dates, capacities, statuses. If it's mono, a source link must exist — the wordmark is the single exception, because the brand itself is a quote from the record. Sentence case everywhere — including headings. Tabular figures always (font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums).

Buttons

Take the data Read BTW Weekly

One primary button per view. Primary is ink, never green — green belongs to verification.

Charts

The zero axis is the meter line (2px ink). Announced/projected data is always ink-3 gray. Verified data is always green. Violations always red. Bars ≤24px, 3px radius on the data end only. No gridlines heavier than --line. No legends when direct labels fit.

Motion

Two animations exist: hero numbers count up once on load (600ms, ease-out), and feed rows stamp in on scroll (opacity + 4px rise, 200ms, staggered). Nothing else moves. Seriousness is stillness.

06 · Voice

Short declaratives. Numbers before adjectives. Every claim carries its receipt. Dry wit allowed once per page — usually in the BTW construction: "BTW, the turbines were running nine months before the permit."

DO

"Operating capacity: 2.0 GW. That's 2.2% of what was announced." · "The permit record says something more interesting." · "Sourced from air permits, not press releases."

DON'T

"Revolutionary insights into the booming BTM landscape!" · "Game-changing analytics platform" · Exclamation marks. The word "landscape." Hype verbs of any kind.
